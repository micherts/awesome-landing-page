Cafe Overheads

Overhead control is critical to your cafe's stability and profitability.

4 Tricks & Techniques

  1. Establish a budget: When there is no clear budget, there is often no clear future. A well thought out cafe budget will help you limit your costs and plan for the future.
  2. Determine your overheads: Make a detailed list of all overhead expenses, including but not limited to lease fees, electricity bills, insurance premiums, and equipment costs.
  3. Track your overheads: There is little point in creating a budget and recording overheads unless you intend to track and monitor them on a regular basis; it is common for operating expenses to spiral out of control due to a lack of frequent monitoring.
  4. Educate your team: Spend some time training your cafe staff on budgeting and cost awareness; this will allow them to be more accountable for overheads under their control and contribute to maintaining a healthy overhead margin, which means a sustainable bottom line. Share the responsibility and include your team; this will improve team culture, trust, and growth.

3 Home Truths

  1. Profitability will decline: Excessive expense can reduce profitability and make it difficult to stay in business. Given how difficult it can be to make a profit from cafes, you really don't need the additional stress that not knowing your overheads can bring.
  2. Extra Stress: High overheads can put a strain on your cash flow, making it harder to pay invoices and suppliers on time. This is a dangerous slope from which it is difficult to recover.
  3. Reduced competitiveness: If your overheads are consistently high, you may be forced to make shortcuts, raise menu pricing, and cut labour. This frequently results in a decline in client experience, which is never good news.

Selecting Suppliers

There are several variables to consider while choosing cafe suppliers for your business.

4 Tricks & Techniques

  1. Sustainability & Quality: Make sure the products you are offering to your customers are of good quality. Take into account the standard of the food, beverages, and other products that potential suppliers are offering. Customers are growing more and more interested in cafes that put sustainability first. Take into account suppliers who emphasise sustainable sourcing or provide eco-friendly items.
  2. Reliability: Is the supplier capable of consistently delivering products on time? Can they fulfil your exact order requirements? Look for cafe suppliers who have a good reputation for dependability. You will thank yourself!
  3. Price: While you don't necessarily want to sacrifice quality for cheaper prices, you do want to be sure you're getting a fair deal. Compare prices from several cafe suppliers and choose the ones in keeping with your business budget. As the saying goes, "Champagne taste with beer money."
  4. Customer service: Take into account how potential cafe suppliers manage customer service. Will they reply to queries and issues quickly? Will they be simple, easy, and "nice" to deal with when you ask for solutions and alternatives to suit your cafe menu?

3 Home Truths

  1. Poor quality products: If you choose a supplier that offers low-quality products, it can lead to dissatisfied customers who may not return to your cafe. Second grade products can also damage your cafe's reputation and harm your business in the short and long term. Not a good thing!
  2. Unreliable deliveries: If your supplier fails to deliver products on time, it may disrupt your cafe's operations and result in inconsistent service. This can result in unhappy consumers who may switch to your competition.
  3. Environmental concerns: It is important to align your core values with your suppliers. Likewise, if your suppliers do not prioritise sustainability or engage in environmentally detrimental practises, your cafe's reputation may suffer and environmentally conscious customers may be turned off.

Cafe Crew and Culture

Developing a positive team culture in your cafe is critical to the success of your business.

4 Tricks & Techniques

  1. Lead by example: As the owner or manager of the cafe, you set the tone for the entire team. Be positive, respectful, and supportive in your interactions with your employees, and they will be more likely to follow your lead.
  2. Communicate effectively: Regular communication with your team is essential for building trust and fostering positive relationships. Encourage open dialogue, listen actively to your employees, and provide constructive feedback.
  3. Encourage teamwork: Foster a sense of teamwork by encouraging collaboration, sharing responsibilities, and promoting a positive team spirit. Encourage your cafe crew to support and help each other, and recognise their contributions and achievements.
  4. Provide opportunities for growth: Offer training and development opportunities to help your employees grow and develop their skills. This can help increase job satisfaction and motivation, and lead to better overall performance.

3 Home Truths

  1. High employee turnover: If your team culture is negative or toxic, employees are more likely to leave, resulting in high turnover rates. This can be costly for your cafe in terms of recruitment, training, and lost productivity.
  2. Poor productivity: A negative team culture can lead to poor productivity, as employees may be demotivated, disengaged, or distracted. This can impact customer service, quality, and overall performance.
  3. Poor service and brand damage: Employees that are disgruntled or disengaged are less likely to give excellent customer service. Customer complaints, poor reviews, and lost revenue can all result from this. Unhappy employees may speak negatively about your cafe to others, resulting in a poor team culture. This can be detrimental to your business in the long term, making it more difficult to attract and keep customers.

Operations and Management

Managing a cafe can be challenging, and there are several important factors to consider.

4 Tricks & Techniques

  1. Staffing: Having the right number of staff with the right skills and experience is essential for running a successful cafe. You need to ensure that you have enough staff to handle busy periods and provide excellent customer service, while also managing costs effectively.
  2. Inventory management: Proper stock control management is critical for cost control and waste reduction. To avoid waste, you must keep track of inventory levels, order supplies on time, and manage stock rotation.
  3. Financial management: Probably the most essential feature and critical to your cafe's success. It is critical to keep track of income, expenses, and cash flow and use this data to make informed decisions about pricing, cost management, and investment.
  4. Customer service: Providing excellent customer service is crucial for building a loyal customer base and attracting new business. Train your staff to be friendly, knowledgeable, and efficient in their interactions with customers.

3 Home Truths

  1. Staff turnover: Poor management practices can lead to staff turnover, which can be costly in terms of recruitment, training, and lost productivity.
  2. Damage to your brand: Poor management and operations can harm your brand's reputation and make it difficult to attract and retain customers. Outstanding operations begin at the top, therefore be sure to take a role in establishing exceptional operations as well as educating individuals to meet your expectations. Document repeatable processes.
  3. Failure to learn: It's important to remember that making mistakes is a natural part of the learning process, and it's okay to not get everything right all the time. The important thing is to learn from your mistakes, identify areas for improvement, and work to improve your understanding of the subject matter going forward.

Menu Development

Menu creation is an important component of running a cafe.

4 Tricks & Techniques

  1. Know your audience: You need to first understand your target market in order to build a menu that will appeal to them. Consider their preferences, dietary needs, and probable spending range while developing your cafe's menu. Pay close attention to your creative abilities when building your menu. You can be creative, but don't alienate your clients by showcasing trendy menu options with painfully long wait times.
  2. Offer a variety of options: Offering a range of cafe menu options, including vegetarian, vegan, and gluten-free items, can help attract a wider customer base and increase customer satisfaction.
  3. Keep it simple: A menu that is concise and well-curated may be more appealing than one that is lengthy and may overwhelm customers. Concentrate on quality above quantity. You do not have to be everything to everyone. Don't compare yourself too much to your competitors; after all, how do you know they've done their homework?
  4. Use seasonal and local ingredients. Incorporating seasonal and local products, where available, can help you stay fresh and produce distinctive recipes that set you apart from your competition. Not everyone enjoys eating tempura prawns from Thailand when they can be purchased in Australia at a reasonable price. Showcase the fact that you use Australian produce in your cafe; it's an excellent look!

  1. Increased competition: If your menu lacks variety and appeal, you may face increased competition from other caf√©s that do. How often will the same customer return for a Bacon & Egg roll and a flat white?
  2. Damage to your cafes brand and reputation: A poorly constructed menu may damage your brand's reputation by generating negative reviews and word-of-mouth. You'll need to think about this one carefully; you won't get all of your inspiration from a cookbook.
  3. Cost increases: Poor menu development and lack of understanding of continuing expenses and cogs-related labour can lead to cost increases due to overstocking ingredients, food waste, and decreased profitability.

Choosing and Using Ingredients

Choosing and using the right ingredients is crucial for the success of any cafe operation.

4 Tricks & Techniques

  1. Consistency: When it comes to ingredients and now they are used within recipes, consistency is very important. To retain the quality and taste of your food, use the same elements every time. Customers will want to know that they will get the same experience they got last time, therefore your cooks and chefs must understand what they are working with and how to handle it so that consumers leave happy.
  2. Sustainability: Think about using sustainable ingredients to attract ecologically aware customers. If you choose to supply sustainable ingredients, you should let people know. We are all getting more environmentally conscious these days, so don't hide the fact that your cafe is contributing!
  3. Cost: When selecting ingredients, price is an essential consideration. However, it is essential to strike a balance between quality and affordability in order to provide items that are both tasty and economical. employing cross-utilisation tactics, such as using staples in a variety of dishes, can help in keeping prep easy, expenses low, and contribute to a healthy bottom line for your cafe.
  4. Allergens and dietary constraints: These have definitely been on the rise recently! Be aware of common allergens and dietary restrictions, and provide options for consumers who have unique dietary requirements. The last thing you want is to spend all of that time designing "the perfect menu" just to have a customer change it every time it's ordered. The kitchen will not thank you, and the customer will most likely feel uncomfortable asking you to cut away at your masterpieces!

3 Home Truths

  1. Legal and regulatory issues: Fines and legal action may be imposed for failing to adhere to legal and regulatory standards, such as the correct labeling of allergies. Over the years, this has brought down some significant F&B players. There's no reason to include your cafe in the horror stories! When labeling your takeaway menu package and in-house menu, be absolutely accurate.
  2. Reduced customer satisfaction: Poor ingredient selection and application can result in dishes that are unappealing, lack flavour, or fail to meet the nutritional needs of clients, resulting in decreased customer satisfaction and lost business.
  3. A shortage of cafe specials: If you want to keep the chefs engaged and motivated, think about hosting regular menu development sessions where they can try out new ingredients and techniques. You can additionally motivate them to be creative by encouraging them to produce cafe specials on occasion.
